The summit meeting between Kim Jong-un and President Trump in Vietnam ended in failure today after the North Korean leader demanded an end to sanctions in return for steps towards denuclearisation.

Mr Trump and his secretary of state, Mike Pompeo, insisted that the relationship with Mr Kim remained warm and friendly and said that they were optimistic about a future agreement.

However, the abrupt cancellation of a celebratory lunch and a ceremony to sign a joint statement is a setback for the president, hours after his own former lawyer called him a conman, racist and a cheat in a congressional hearing.
